Connie Donohue says trees are taking over her block. She’s spent four decades at the same home in Marine Park — a quiet, parkside corner of south Brooklyn. For about as long, she recalls roots unearthing sidewalks and creeping into the sewers; sprawling branches growing over stop signs.

“It’s a problem. A lot of the trees are overgrown,” Donohue said. “There’s a general very bad feeling about the tree maintenance in the neighborhood.”

Donohue isn’t alone. A new data investigation reveals that overgrown trees are a chronic concern in the Marine Park neighborhood. And residents interviewed for this story worry their concerns have gone unheard by the New York City Department of Parks & Recreation, which is charged with maintaining trees on public property.
